Every plant carries a hidden language written in its DNA — a chemistry shaped by millions of years of survival in harsh environments.

I am a plant scientist who reads this language in the native flora of Saudi Arabia. Our plants have evolved a remarkable arsenal of secondary metabolites — natural molecules that helped them survive heat, drought, and salt, and that today hold promise as future medicines, anticancer agents, and bio-based products.

To uncover them, I combine three layers of biological data — a plant's genome (its DNA blueprint), its transcriptome (the mRNA showing which genes are switched on), and its metabolome (the metabolites a plant actually produces). I then apply bioinformatics — using computers to make sense of biological data — to trace each compound from its DNA origin to the molecule itself.

Aligned with Saudi Vision 2030, my work pursues three commitments:

  • Bioprospecting — systematically searching Saudi plants for valuable natural products that can advance medicine, biotechnology, and the bioeconomy.
  • Conservation — using genomic tools to protect Saudi plant biodiversity and build resilient landscapes for a changing climate.
  • People & Society — training a new generation of Saudi scientists and sharing plant science with the wider community to inform public awareness and traditional knowledge of our flora.
